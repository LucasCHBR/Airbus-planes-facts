A300

The Airbus A300 is a wide-body airliner designed and produced by Airbus. It was introduced in 1972 and was the first aircraft produced by the company.

The A300 typically seats up to 266 passengers and has a range of up to 6,300 km. It is powered by two engines, and is known for its efficiency and versatility.

A310

The Airbus A310 is a wide-body airliner designed and produced by Airbus. It was introduced in 1982 and is a shorter and lighter version of the A300.

The A310 typically seats up to 220 passengers and has a range of up to 9,500 km. It is powered by two engines, and is known for its fuel efficiency and reliability.

A318

The Airbus A318 is a narrow-body airliner designed and produced by Airbus. It was introduced in 2003 and is the smallest member of the A320 family.

The A318 typically seats up to 132 passengers and has a range of up to 5,700 km. It is powered by two engines, and is known for its fuel efficiency and versatility.

A319

The Airbus A319 is a narrow-body airliner designed and produced by Airbus. It was introduced in 1996 and is a shorter version of the A320.

The A319 typically seats up to 160 passengers and has a range of up to 6,700 km. It is powered by two engines, and is known for its efficiency, reliability, and ease of maintenance.

A320

The A320 series has two variants, the A320-100 and A320-200. Only 21 A320-100s were produced.

These aircraft, the first to be manufactured, were delivered to Air Inter – later acquired by Air France – and British Airways as a result of an order from British Caledonian made prior to its acquisition.

A321

As the A320 was beginning operations in 1988, the A321 was launched as its first derivative the same year.

The A321 fuselage is stretched by 6.93 metres (22 ft 9 in), with a 4.27 m (14 ft 0 in) front plug immediately forward of wing and a 2.67 m (8 ft 9 in) rear plug.

A330

Airbus conceived several derivatives of the A300, its first airliner from the mid-1970s. Then the company began development on the A330 twinjet

The A330-300, the first variant, took its maiden flight in November 1992 and entered service with Air Inter in January 1994. The slightly shorter A330-200 variant followed in 1998.

A340

In the mid-1970s, Airbus conceived several derivatives of the A300, its first airliner, and developed the A340 quadjet in parallel with the A330 twinjet.

In June 1987, Airbus launched both designs with their first orders and the A340-300 took its maiden flight on 25 October 1991. It was certified along with the A340-200 on 22 December 1992 and both versions entered service in March 1993 with launch customers Lufthansa and Air France.

A350

The first A350 design proposed by Airbus in 2004, in response to the Boeing 787 Dreamliner, would have been a development of the A330 with composite wings and new engines.

As market support was inadequate, in 2006, Airbus switched to a clean-sheet "XWB" (eXtra Wide Body) design, powered by two Rolls-Royce Trent XWB turbofan engines.

A380

The A380 is the world's largest passenger airliner and only full-length double-deck jet airliner.

Airbus studies started in 1988, and the project was announced in 1990 to challenge the dominance of the Boeing 747 in the long-haul market.
